Timothy Lutheran Church

Website Photo Collection Guide

Prepared for ministry leaders — March 2026
We are building a new church website at timothystl.org. Many of the pages are complete, but need real photos to bring them to life. This guide tells you exactly what kinds of photos we need, where each one goes, and what to aim for when you collect them. Phone photos are fine — modern smartphones take excellent photos. No professional photographer required.
Priority 1

Page Header (Hero) Photos

These are the large banner images at the top of each page — the first thing a visitor sees. The code is already waiting for these files. Once you drop a photo in, I can activate it with one change. Wide, landscape-orientation photos work best.

Page File to Deliver What to Photograph Tips
/music music-hero.jpg Choir, handbells, or the organ during a service or rehearsal Wide shot. Include people, not just instruments.
/stephen stephen-hero.jpg Two people in a caring conversation — warm and personal Candid is better than posed. Coffee or a table setting adds warmth.
/foodpantry foodpantry-hero.jpg Volunteers actively working in the pantry Action shot — sorting produce, stocking shelves, handing off bags.
/bees bees-hero.jpg Beekeepers in gear at the hives, preferably outdoors Wide exterior if possible. Two people together is more inviting than solo.
/worship worship-hero.jpg Full sanctuary from the back — congregation + lit altar Shoot during a service. Natural light plus sanctuary lighting is ideal.
/home (optional) home-hero.jpg Exterior of the building, or a neighborhood street scene on Fyler Golden hour (early morning or evening) gives the building warmth.
Technical note: Photos will have a dark color overlay applied on top, so the text "Timothy Lutheran Church" can be read over them. This means slightly underexposed or moody photos work well. Very bright, high-contrast white scenes don't work as well. Aim for at least 1,200 pixels wide — larger is always better.
Priority 2

Ministry Page Content Photos

These sit inside the page body as featured images — larger format, higher detail than a hero shot. They help visitors understand what the ministry actually looks like in practice.

Page What to Photograph Notes
/music Close-up of choir or a specific instrument — organ pipes, handbells on a table, guitarist mid-song Individual musician at work. Detail and texture are good here.
/stephen Stephen minister with a care receiver in conversation, or volunteers in a training session Authenticity matters more than production quality.
/foodpantry Volunteers packing bags, produce on tables, the pantry shelves stocked, families receiving food Aim for 2–3 photos. The pantry logo image already exists — this supplements it.
/bees Close-up of honeycomb or bees on a frame; someone lifting a frame; honey being harvested You already have a photo — collect 1–2 additional angles if possible.
/christmasmarket Market day crowd, vendor booths, festive lighting, children at the market You already have one photo. Collect 2–3 more for a future gallery addition.
/wol School hallway, classroom with students, kids working at desks A real building/student photo would strengthen this page. Coordinate with Word of Life administration.

Priority 4

Video Content

Short videos can replace or complement text on several pages. These don't need to be produced — a steady phone video with decent audio is enough for most uses.

Page What to Record Target Length
/worship "What to expect" walkthrough — show the sanctuary, someone being greeted, a brief clip of the service in action. A first-time visitor should feel like they know what Sunday morning looks like. 60–90 seconds
/foodpantry Short clip of volunteers working — powerful for donors and potential volunteers. Even 30 seconds of authentic activity is compelling. 30–60 seconds
/christmasmarket Reel of the market in action — crowd, booths, music, kids. Collect footage this December for the 2026 page update. 60–90 seconds
/sermons Already links to your YouTube channel — ensure the channel is current and playlists are organized by series. Ongoing
/wol A YouTube embed from the Word of Life school already exists — confirm with WOL administration that the video is current and appropriate. Existing video
Priority 5

Congregation & Community Photos

These aren't assigned to a specific page yet, but they're the most powerful photos on any church website — they answer the question every first-time visitor asks: "What kind of people go here?" Collect these over several Sundays.

Subject What to Capture
Sunday morning life People arriving, greeting each other in the narthex, coffee hour conversations, children in the hallway
Intergenerational moments Older members with children and young families together; multi-ethnic groups — reflect the actual congregation
Worship moments Communion, baptism (with permission), confirmation, candlelight service
The neighborhood Fyler Ave exterior, Lindenwood Park street scenes, the building in its context
Seasonal Easter, Advent, Lenten services — the sanctuary changes throughout the year and photos should reflect that
